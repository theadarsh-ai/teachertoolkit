1. Our Incredible Numbers! (Place Value & Number Sense)

The population of a beautiful town in Uttar Pradesh is 8,35,709.

  • a) Write this number in words according to the Indian Place Value System.
  • b) What is the place value of the digit '3' in this number?

---

2. Delicious Diwali Delights! (Addition & Subtraction)

During Diwali, Priya’s family prepared a big batch of sweets. They made 275 Gulab Jamuns and 198 Jalebis. On the day of Diwali, they distributed 150 sweets to their neighbours and relatives.

  • How many sweets did they make in total?
  • How many sweets were left after distributing some?

---

3. Cricket Field Calculations! (Measurement & Multiplication)

A standard cricket pitch is 22 meters long.

  • a) How many centimeters long is the cricket pitch? (Hint: 1 meter = 100 centimeters)
  • b) If a cricketer runs 4 full lengths of the pitch (from one end to the other, and back 3 times), how many meters has he run in total?

---

4. Sharing festive Ladoos! (Fractions)

Rohan brought a box of 12 delicious Boondi Ladoos for his friends. He gave $\frac{1}{4}$ of the ladoos to Aryan and $\frac{1}{3}$ of the ladoos to Pooja.

  • a) How many ladoos did Aryan get?
  • b) How many ladoos did Pooja get?
  • c) What fraction of the total ladoos did Rohan distribute in all?

---

5. Market Money Math! (Money & Decimals)

Meena went to the local market (haat) with her mother. They bought fresh vegetables worth ₹215.50 and fruits worth ₹130.75.

  • a) What is the total amount they spent at the market?
  • b) If Meena's mother paid with a ₹500 note, how much change did they get back?

---

6. Drawing a Beautiful Rangoli! (Perimeter & Area)

A beautiful rectangular Rangoli design is drawn on the floor of a house. It is 120 cm long and 80 cm wide.

  • a) What is the perimeter of this Rangoli design? (Hint: Perimeter = 2 x (length + width))
  • b) What is the area covered by the Rangoli design? (Hint: Area = length x width)
