[English Summary of the Story] The Light of Education

There was a little girl named Rina. Rina loved to play a lot. She would play from morning till evening and didn't like studying at all. Whenever her mother asked her to open a book, she would wrinkle her nose and say, "I just want to play!"

Rina's Naani (grandmother) was very wise. She would lovingly explain things to Rina. One day, Naani called Rina to her. "Rina, dear," Naani said, "Do you like listening to stories?"

Rina's eyes lit up. "Yes, Naani! I love stories very much!"

Naani smiled and brought a beautiful, colourful book. "Look Rina," she said, "This book has so many fun stories! Of kings and queens, animals, and even beautiful birds."

Rina was delighted to see the book. "Wow, Naani! This is so nice! Will you read it to me?"

Naani lovingly stroked Rina's head and said, "I will definitely read it to you. But dear, if you study, you can read all these stories yourself. You won't have to wait for anyone."

Rina started thinking. "I can read by myself?"

"Yes, dear," Naani explained. "Education is like a magic lamp. It opens your mind and helps you understand all the beautiful things in the world. When you study, you will learn new words, discover new things, and the world will open up for you. You can read bus, train, or shop signs without anyone's help. Education will help you become whatever you want to be when you grow up."

Rina understood Naani's words. She realized how truly important education was. From then on, Rina started putting her mind into studying, along with playing. She now considered her school books her friends.

And yes, Rina now listened to stories from Naani and also tried to read her own book. With the light of education, her life became even brighter.
